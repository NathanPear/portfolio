PROJECT 7 / MECHANICAL

Nutcracker

A custom mechanical tool designed to securely hold hazelnuts in place for easy breaking.

  • Role: mechanical design and CAD modeling
  • Focus: utility tooling and holding fixtures
  • Constraint: ergonomic and material constraints

Project Details

Problem and Context

Hazelnuts are notoriously difficult to break open cleanly. Traditional hand nutcrackers require high grip strength and often send shells flying, while using a hammer on a flat surface results in nuts slipping away before impact.

The objective was to design a simple holding fixture that holds hazelnuts in place securely while they are broken open with a hammer, safeguarding fingers and containing debris.

Scope and Constraints
  • Secure hazelnut positioning for hammer impact
  • Prevent nuts from slipping or rolling out
  • Durable structure to withstand repeated impacts

Approach

What I Built
  • A mechanical block tool featuring a specialized V-groove holding nest
  • Autodesk Inventor design built to handle various hazelnut shapes
  • Durable base plate designed to be struck safely with a hammer
Cross-Disciplinary Read

This project is a practical application of fixture design. Rather than designing a complex mechanism with moving parts, the nutcracker utilizes static geometry to solve a dynamic safety and alignment issue.

Outcome

Result

The resulting nutcracker block securely cradles nuts during cracking, dramatically reducing slipping and protecting users' fingers from accidental hammer blows.

Takeaways
  • Fixture design is a crucial subclass of mechanical engineering
  • Simple tools can solve everyday safety problems effectively
