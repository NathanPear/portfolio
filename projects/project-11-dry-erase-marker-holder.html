PROJECT 11 / MECHANICAL

Dry Erase Marker Holder

A simple 3D-printed storage and organizer tray for dry erase markers.

  • Role: CAD design and utility prototyping
  • Focus: organizer layout and clearance tolerances
  • Constraint: standard marker sizes and orientation

Project Details

Problem and Context

Dry erase markers left lying horizontally or upside down in random places get lost easily, and storing them with tips up causes them to dry out quickly. A dedicated storage tray was needed to keep them organized and stored in the ideal orientation.

The goal was to design a simple, utility-focused organizer tray to hold and store dry erase markers cleanly on a magnetic board or desk.

Scope and Constraints
  • Sized to fit standard dry erase marker diameters
  • Clean, compact slots for quick insertion and removal
  • 3D-printed model designed to be robust and light

Approach

What I Built
  • A 3D-printed organizer tray with individual slots for dry erase markers
  • Custom Autodesk Inventor model optimized for clean printing and slot clearances
  • Sturdy base structure suitable for desk or board attachment
Cross-Disciplinary Read

Though a simple organizer, this project is a good example of utility design. Designing objects for specific fit and clearance is a core part of creating usable tool holders and fixtures in any workshop environment.

Outcome

Result

The resulting holder organizes dry erase markers cleanly, ensuring they are stored correctly and kept within easy reach.

Takeaways
  • Even simple utility items benefit from precise CAD modeling and clearance checking
  • Designing for daily utility ensures high product usability
