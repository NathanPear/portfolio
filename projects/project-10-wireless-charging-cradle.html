PROJECT 10 / MECHANICAL

Wireless Charging Cradle

A custom wireless charging cradle optimized for specific phone and charger geometry.

  • Role: CAD design and functional prototyping
  • Focus: cradle geometry and fit tolerances
  • Constraint: phone case thickness and charger alignment

Project Details

Problem and Context

Generic phone stands often align poorly with wireless charging coils, leading to slow charging or complete failure. Furthermore, standard stands rarely accommodate phone cases well, requiring users to remove them before charging.

The objective was to design a charging cradle sized specifically for a particular wireless charger and my phone with its case on, ensuring optimal coil alignment and easy drop-in use.

Scope and Constraints
  • Sized perfectly for specific wireless charger dimensions
  • Accommodate the phone thickness with its protective case
  • Maintain correct alignment with the charging coil

Approach

What I Built
  • A custom charging cradle that integrates the wireless charger block
  • Autodesk Inventor model built around exact phone and case tolerances
  • Slanted stand design for optimal screen viewing while charging
Cross-Disciplinary Read

This project is a classic packaging problem. It requires measuring and modeling existing physical components (the phone, the case, and the charger) and creating a new housing that integrates them seamlessly.

Outcome

Result

The resulting cradle provides reliable wireless charging by holding the phone and charger in perfect alignment, allowing for easy drop-in charging without removing the case.

Takeaways
  • Accurate physical measurements are critical when designing custom enclosures
  • Drop-in convenience requires careful tolerance management
