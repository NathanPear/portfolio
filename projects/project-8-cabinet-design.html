PROJECT 8 / MECHANICAL

Cabinet Design

A parametric cabinet assembly design prepared in Autodesk Inventor.

  • Role: CAD modeling and parametric assembly
  • Focus: constraint-based CAD design
  • Constraint: high school class standards and assembly fit

Project Details

Problem and Context

Prepared as a major design project in high school, this cabinet served as an introduction to parametric modeling. The objective was to design a functional, multi-compartment cabinet that matches standard furniture tolerances.

Scope and Constraints
  • Develop clean parametric constraints
  • Ensure correct assembly alignment of drawers and doors
  • Apply realistic material appearances and finishes

Approach

What I Built
  • A fully constrained 3D assembly model of a cabinet in Autodesk Inventor
  • Functional drawers and doors mapped with geometric constraints
  • My first major CAD assembly project demonstrating fundamental design logic
Cross-Disciplinary Read

This project shows the transition from basic geometric sketches to parametric, multi-component design. Understanding how individual parts constrain to one another is foundational to any complex mechanical assembly.

Outcome

Result

The cabinet model was successfully designed, constrained, and rendered, serving as a baseline for all subsequent parametric CAD projects.

Takeaways
  • Parametric modeling saves significant time when modifying dimensions
  • Proper constraint logic is key to building stable assemblies
