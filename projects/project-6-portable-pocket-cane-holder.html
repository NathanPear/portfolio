PROJECT 6 / MECHANICAL

Portable Pocket Cane Holder

A collapsible mobility-aid C-frame table clamp with a variable fulcrum and detent locking system.

  • Role: mechanism design, kinematics, and prototyping
  • Focus: collapsible structures and adjustable force
  • Constraint: portability and table thickness variation

Project Details

Problem and Context

Mobility aid users often lack a place to secure their canes at tables or counters, leading to canes falling or creating tripping hazards. Existing holders are either permanent fixtures or too bulky for portability.

The design challenge was to create a product capable of securing a cane to a table while remaining small enough to be portable, and easy enough to use to be practical.

Scope and Constraints
  • Collapsible design to fit inside a pocket
  • Secure clamping on table edges of varying thicknesses
  • Easy operation with limited hand strength
  • Maintain rigid configuration under clamping tension

Approach

What I Built
  • Kinematic Folding Design: three-member frame with free-swinging hinges that collapse into a nested plane
  • Detent Locking Mechanism: custom orthogonal rods and rack lock with a spring-loaded release button
  • Variable-Length Cantilever Spring: sliding fulcrum adjusts effective spring length of the leaf member
  • Adjustable clamping force tuned for different counter thicknesses
Cross-Disciplinary Read

This design combines folding kinematic links with structural spring logic. By shifting the pivot point of the leaf-spring arm, it allows the user to tune the mechanical advantage and spring stiffness to match different tables.

This project is legible to reviewers interested in collapsible mechanisms, mobility aids, product engineering, and functional prototypes.

Outcome

Result

The resulting portable cane holder folds flat for pocket transport and clamps securely onto table edges between 0.5" and 2.0" thick.

The combination of the detent lock and leaf spring provides a stable clamp that prevents cane falls without adding unnecessary bulk.

Takeaways
  • Folding kinematics must handle both force transmission and space efficiency
  • Spring stiffness can be adjustably tuned through geometric constraints rather than material changes
  • Hinged linkages must lock reliably to prevent collapse under load
