PROJECT 9 / MECHANICAL

Compliant Table Cane Holder

A table-edge clip utilizing compliant mechanism design to secure a cane.

  • Role: compliant mechanism design and 3D printing
  • Focus: compliant structures and snap-fits
  • Constraint: table edge constraints and material flexibility

Project Details

Problem and Context

Mobility aid users need simple ways to park their canes at tables. Traditional spring-clamp designs require complex assemblies, pins, and metal springs, which increase cost and complexity.

The objective was to design a single-piece compliant mechanism cane holder that snaps onto a specific table edge and allows a cane to securely click in place.

Scope and Constraints
  • Single-piece design printable on standard 3D printers
  • Secure mechanical snap-fit for standard cane diameters
  • Appropriate material thickness to support flexure without yielding

Approach

What I Built
  • A compliant mechanism cane holder tailored for a specific table edge
  • A semicircular snap clip that utilizes material elasticity to secure the cane
  • A single-body 3D-printed model that eliminates separate spring assemblies
Cross-Disciplinary Read

This design utilizes the flexural properties of the 3D-printed material to act as a spring, demonstrating key principles of compliant mechanisms. It replaces an assembly of parts with a single jointless structure.

Outcome

Result

The compliant cane holder successfully secures standard canes to the target table, using a simple snap-in motion and holding them stable.

Takeaways
  • Compliant mechanisms can replace multi-part assemblies, reducing component count
  • Understanding material flexural limits is key to avoiding plastic deformation
